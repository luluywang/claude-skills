## [intro.tex]

### Anglo-Saxon over Latin & Weak Verbs

### - [ ] Line 20: "I estimate that consumers are ten times more sensitive"
**Comment:** Weak framing "I estimate that" is unnecessarily passive. Could be "Consumers are ten times more sensitive" — the "I" is already implied by the paper.
**Original:**
```
I estimate that consumers are ten times more sensitive to rewards than merchants are to fees.
```
**Proposed Revision:**
```
Consumers are ten times more sensitive to rewards than merchants are to fees.
```
**Why better:** Removes unnecessary "I estimate"; the paper is the context, and the claim stands stronger without self-reference

---

### - [ ] Line 28: "giving them incentives to promote"
**Comment:** "Giving incentives to promote" is Latin/wordy. Should be "incentivize."
**Original:**
```
Networks charge merchants interchange fees and pay the revenue to banks, giving them incentives to promote the networks' cards through consumer rewards and other forms of steering.
```
**Proposed Revision:**
```
Networks charge merchants interchange fees and pay the revenue to banks, incentivizing card promotion through consumer rewards and steering.
```
**Why better:** "Incentivize" is one word instead of "giving incentives to"; "card promotion" is more concise than "promote the networks' cards"

---

### - [ ] Line 31: "Accepting cards significantly increases merchant sales"
**Comment:** "Increases" is acceptable but "raises" is more Anglo-Saxon and direct in economics writing.
**Original:**
```
Second, accepting cards significantly increases merchant sales.
```
**Proposed Revision:**
```
Card acceptance raises merchant sales.
```
**Why better:** "Raises" is standard in economics; tighter and removes enumeration artifact

### - [ ] Line 47: "conduct an analysis" construction embedded in "I estimate consumer and merchant preferences by matching"
**Comment:** Not a perfect "conduct an analysis" but similar weakness in "by matching the reduced-form facts, moments from payment surveys, and aggregate market-level data." Could be more direct: "using reduced-form facts, survey moments, and market data."
**Original:**
```
I estimate consumer and merchant preferences by matching the reduced-form facts, moments from payment surveys, and aggregate market-level data.
```
**Proposed Revision:**
```
I estimate consumer and merchant preferences using reduced-form facts, survey moments, and market-level data.

**Why better:** "Using" is tighter than "by matching"; shorter noun phrases ("survey moments" not "moments from payment surveys"; "market-level data" not "aggregate market-level data")

## [conclusion.tex]

### - [ ] Lines 9-10: Weak verb "speak to"

**Comment:** "The counterfactual results speak to contemporary legal and policy debates" uses the weak, colloquial phrase "speak to." In academic prose, the stronger verb is "address" or "engage with" or "illuminate."

**Original:**
```
The counterfactual results speak to contemporary legal and policy debates in payments and platform markets.
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
The counterfactual results illuminate contemporary legal and policy debates in payments and platform markets.
```

**Why better:** "Illuminate" is more precise and direct than the colloquial "speak to." It clarifies the relationship: the results shed light on the debates rather than merely "speaking" to them.

---

### - [ ] Lines 23-24: Redundant framing "which is the harm that"

**Comment:** "A monopoly network raises per-transaction fees, which is the harm that antitrust cases have targeted" uses the redundant construction "which is the harm that." Simpler: "A monopoly network raises per-transaction fees — the harm antitrust cases target."

**Original:**
```
A monopoly network raises per-transaction fees, which is the harm that antitrust cases have targeted.
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
A monopoly network raises per-transaction fees — the primary target of antitrust cases.
```

**Why better:** Removes redundant relative clause structure ("which is... that") and tightens the connection between the fee increase and its antitrust significance.

---

### - [ ] Lines 28-29: Weak verb "targets"

**Comment:** "Antitrust typically targets harms from low output" uses "targets" as a weak verb choice. The more direct verb is "addresses" or "focuses on."

**Original:**
```
Antitrust typically targets harms from low output.
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
Antitrust typically addresses harms from low output.
```

**Why better:** "Addresses" is more precise — antitrust laws are designed to address (solve/remedy) harms, not just "target" (aim at) them.

## [appendix_credit_debit.tex]

### - [ ] Lines 62: Weak verb "explores"

**Comment:** "Explore how relaxing" is vague and tentative. Economics writing benefits from more direct action verbs.

**Original:**
```
Below, I explore how relaxing the no substitution assumption changes the model.
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
Below, I relax the no substitution assumption and derive how it changes the model.
```

**Why better:** "Relax and derive" is more direct and concrete than "explore how." It matches the technical action being taken.

### - [ ] Lines 98–99: Weak verb and nominalization

**Comment:** "The model needs parameters" and "scale the sales increase" use passive construction and nominalization. More direct phrasing.

**Original:**
```
When debit generates fewer incremental sales than credit, the model needs parameters $\kappa_j$ for each payment method that scale the sales increase relative to credit cards.
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
When debit generates fewer incremental sales than credit, we parameterize each payment method with $\kappa_j$ to weight its sales boost relative to credit cards.
```

**Why better:** "Parameterize" and "weight" are more concrete and active than "the model needs parameters" and "scale the sales increase."

### - [ ] Lines 139–141: Redundant framing

**Comment:** "A two-card example makes this dependence visible" is vague framing. The phrase "makes visible" is weaker than direct statement.

**Original:**
```
A two-card example makes this dependence visible.
Keep the convention from Online Appendix~\ref{subsec:oa-credit-multihoming} that card~$1$ is debit (fee $\tau_d$, sales parameter $\kappa_d\leq1$) and card~$2$ is credit (fee $\tau_c$, sales parameter $\kappa_c = 1$).
```

**Proposed Revision:**
```
A two-card example demonstrates this dependence.
Let card~$1$ denote debit (fee $\tau_d$, sales parameter $\kappa_d\leq1$) and card~$2$ denote credit (fee $\tau_c$, sales parameter $\kappa_c = 1$), following Online Appendix~\ref{subsec:oa-credit-multihoming}.

**Why better:** "Demonstrates" is more direct than "makes visible." "Let X denote Y" is clearer than "Keep the convention that X is Y."

### - [ ] Lines 206–208: Weak verb in substantive claim

**Comment:** "Depends on" is a passive linking verb. Strengthen with direct causation.

**Original:**
```
The threshold~\eqref{eq:general-credit-debit-threshold} contains $D = \zeta M$.

**Proposed Revision:**
```
The threshold~\eqref{eq:general-credit-debit-threshold} contains $D = \zeta M$.
When $\zeta > 0$, credit-debit multi-homing rates enter the credit acceptance threshold.

**Why better:** "Enter" is more concrete and active than "the threshold depends on." It specifies that multi-homing rates directly affect the calculation.

### - [ ] Lines 310–311: Dense nominalization

**Why better:** "Provide the resolution" is more direct than "runs through." "Favor" and "forcing" are stronger than "weight" and "requires."
