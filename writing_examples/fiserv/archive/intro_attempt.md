Visa and Mastercard tax merchants to subsidize banks.
Every time a merchant accepts a card transaction, the card issuer collects a fee known as the interchange fee.
In 2022, U.S. merchants paid around \$100 billion in interchange.
These fees are opaque: they vary by card type, sector, and merchant size, with larger merchants often negotiating lower rates.

Interchange has motivated decades of litigation and legislation.
The 2011 Durbin Amendment capped debit interchange in the U.S.; in 2022 Senator Dick Durbin proposed the Credit Card Competition Act in an attempt to bring down interchange fees, and a pending 2025 settlement over interchange fees and premium credit cards in particular is a continuation of a lawsuit led by Walmart over two decades ago \citep{Andriotis2025}.
Globally, E.U. regulations have set interchange fees at less than one-tenth of U.S. levels.

Despite this policy attention, the incidence of interchange fees remains unclear.
Merchant groups call interchange a hidden tax extracted from the economy (Merchant Payment Coalition), while banks frame it as the necessary cost of funding rewards (BPI).
The academic literature recognizes that both of these perspectives are incomplete.
When merchants pass on interchange fees into higher retail prices and banks pass on interchange fees to rewards, interchange fees ultimately redistribute from cash and debit users to credit card users \citep{carlton1994antitrust}.

Estimates of the size of the transfer differ widely.
Whereas the arguments made by \citet{Schuh2010, Felt.etal2023} suggest transfers of around \$40 billion dollars per year transferred from cash and debit card users to credit card users, \citet{Gans2018} argues that consumer sorting across merchants may substantially limit cross-subsidization.
Without merchant-level data on payment composition and actual fees -- including negotiated rates -- these positions have proven difficult to test.

In this paper, we provide new evidence on the incidence of interchange fees using a unique merchant-level dataset from Fiserv.
